Photos

Many of us have attended a wedding that is just too long. Hours, even days long. Your head hurts, but the show must go on. All that noise, Roman would just want to go home to read his book alone.

That's not exactly what we had, but the the shear quantity of planning, rescheduling and the number of "weddings" we've had is certainly enough for both of our lifetimes. All of this because our two lovely families got divided not only by the ocean, but also by the dread virus.

So, to those of you who don't know what a wedding during pandemic entails, buckle your seats and let's travel down memory lane.

The Engagement

The story begins with a 4600 km (2800 mile) long trip from Vancouver to Toronto. Karen drove the couple across Canada in 4 days. And Roman prepared a surprise for her. In the mountains of the Yoho National Park, Roman proposed. And she said YES!

The Barefoot backyard wedding

Next summer Karen’s closest family gathered in the backyard to witness an informal barefoot backyard wedding ceremony with beautiful flowers from Karen’s mom Chaz.

To ensure guests’ safety, we were outside the whole time. And those of you who know August Toronto weather will understand why the wedding dinner quickly became The Wedding of Many Hats :)

These photos feature Karen’s parents Phil and Chaz, Karen’s aunt Marg and her daughter Katie.

Winter Photoshoot

The wedding celebration was planned to happen in snowy January. Our website featured a count down to the wedding celebration for a long time (we were excited). Back in April it seemed that the pandemic is almost over and will surely be gone by the end of summer. Alas folks plan, and God laughs.

Due to the travel restrictions the photoshoot from Oleh Bozhyk could be attended only by Roman’s best man, Ruben, and two of Karen’s bridesmaids, Katie and Kate. Well, of course Karen’s family was there with us as well. Karen’s hair was self-styled, whereas makeup was entrusted to Kate

After the photoshoot we were all trying to get cozy and warm next to the fireplace with our tea.

Trip to Ukraine

The trip to Ukraine followed. We are frequent flyers and yet we have never seen Pearson so empty and sad... The religious ceremony was at the Church of Hope, Love and Faith in Volodymyr-Volynski, where Father Eugene, our family spiritual teacher first baptized Karen.

The baptism of an adult Canadian (as opposed to a Ukrainian infant) In the youngest church of Volodymyr-Volynski meant that none of us knew what would happen next. The event was unusual not only for our family, but for the entire town. So Karen and Roman became a news story at the local newspaper: The Ukranian traditional embroidered attire is thanks to Roman’s grandmother Nadiya. Now we are no longer distinguishable by the presence/absence of the cross!

Religious wedding

Wedding celebrations were cancelled in Lutsk (Ukraine) and in Toronto, but the religious marriage happened! Karen’s family stayed in Toronto, but her parents were able to not only meet my family (at least through Zoom), but also to see the entire wedding ceremony Thanks to our cameraman, Roman’s brother Andriy.

The lovely embroidered rushnyk that we are stepping on for the ceremony was embroidered for us by hand by Roman’s Grandmother Eudokia. Roman’s dad Volodymyr was our personal chauffeur and Roman’s mom Svitlana was in charge of guest delivery and a thousand and one important details that Karen and Roman didn’t have to worry about (THANK YOU!) The church was quite cold, so Farther Eugene got the personal heater for Karen and Roman, but the rest of the guests had to wear coats. After the traditional wedding wishes from family and the family photo Karen and Roman are finally officially married both in Canada and in Ukraine in the eyes of Law and God.

As they say in Ireland:
May the road rise up to meet you.
May the wind be always at your back.
May the sun shine warm upon your face;
the rains fall soft upon your fields and until we meet again,
may God hold you in the palm of His hand.

What is the other thing we remember from that day? It was snowing day and night, just like in great Canadian North. Apparently Karen brought Canadian winter with her To put a proper accent on the amount of snow in our backyard, Karen “decided” to conveniently lose her earrings in the snow. So Roman and his dad melted buckets of snow. And actually found one of the earrings! While the other is still hiding in the snow...

We want to thank Roman’s Godmother Liudmyla for a pretty and delicious Korovai (traditional wedding bread)

And Thanks to Roman’s Godparents Sergiy and Liuda for a delicious and pretty cake!

Roman’s other Godmother Oxana organized delicious cupcakes for all of the guests to take home. and helped us get the right flowers for the bride.

The evening of the wedding was a socially distanced dinner with many phone calls and delicious baked duck from Roman’s Grandma Yevdokiya and way too many sweets from everyone. As always, we hope to soon safely celebrate with the rest of our family and friends. We will keep in touch and will continue practicing our dance moves. Sending you all virtual hugs and looking forward to the real ones,
With love,
Karen and Roman
