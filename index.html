Wedding day traditions

Paying the ransom

12 pm

The groom and his party arrive to the bride's house and offer ransom for the bride. The bride's party stands around the entrance with a table, blocking the groom's way. Negotiation starts. The groom offers something cheap at first (alcohol, chocolate, money ...).
Meanwhile kids (usually boys) on the grooms side are urged to sneak into the house to steal a shoe or other bridal item, which will instantly bring negotiations to an end. The groom's party is also urging the bride's party to start counting the money or consume anything (candy, alcohol, etc.) - since such act will also end the negotiation. The bride's party (especially the kids) is warned of this treacherous tactics in advance and furiously resist, insisting the paid amount is way too low.
Everyone is talking over each other and people are having lots of fun! Once the ransom is negotiated, the bride's family allows the groom to enter and see his bride.

Parents' Blessing

1 pm

The young couple kneel on an embroidered ritual cloth (rushnyk) before their parents. The parents hold the icons of Jesus Christ and the Virgin Mary, each draped with a rushnyk. The parents bless their children, wishing them a joyous, prosperous and healthy life together. It is here that the two families officially become one.
This can happen at bride's house or at the courthouse before the ceremony.

Wedding Procession

1:20 pm

The wedding procession usually includes a limousine or a different fancy car for the bride and groom, sometimes a bus for the guests. All the cars are decorated with flowers, stripes, balloons and they honk as they are driving from place to place trying to be as obnoxious and loud as possible.
In order to send the young couple down a happy road of life, parents walk around the wedding procession 3 times with the jars filled with a mixture of candy, coins and wheat grains. They throw the goods on the ground: candy for sweet and careless life, money for prosperity and wheat for good health.
The best part of this tradition is groups of kids competing to collect the best candy and most money.

Civil service

1:30 pm

More detailed description needed here: Close family and wedding party are present (⪡ 50 ppl). Officiant reads some stuff and you exchange vows, sign the marriage certificate.
At the end of the ceremony there is a champagne bang and a young couple drinks some and spills a lot of champagne.

Step on the rushnyk

2:00 pm

The couple steps on a traditional embroidered cloth (rushnyk) before they take their vows. The saying goes: whoever steps on the cloth first will have the final say throughout the marriage.
